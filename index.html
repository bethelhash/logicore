    Methodology & Data Sources

    Freight Class (NMFC)

    Freight class is calculated from cargo density (weight ÷ volume in lbs/ft³) using the NMFC (National Motor Freight Classification) density-to-class table. Classes range from 50 (dense, low-cost) to 500 (lightweight, high-cost). All density-to-class mappings follow NMFC 100 Series guidelines.

    Base Freight Rates

    Indicative base rates are derived from published tariff ranges filed with the US Surface Transportation Board (STB) for LTL/FTL, IATA tariff guides for air freight, and World Container Index (WCI) published rates for ocean. All rates are subject to significant variation based on carrier, lane, volume, and contract terms.

    Import Duties

    Duty rates use the WTO MFN (Most Favoured Nation) published schedules accessed via the WTO Tariff Download Facility and USITC HTS database. Preferential rates under FTAs (USMCA, UK Global Tariff, CPTPP) are noted but not automatically applied — always confirm with your broker.

    CBAM — Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism

    CBAM estimates use the European Commission's published default emission factors per commodity category (EU Reg. 2023/956 Annex III) and the current EU ETS carbon price. CBAM applies to iron/steel, aluminium, cement, fertilisers, electricity, and hydrogen imports to the EU. Full CBAM financial obligations take effect from January 2026.

    3D Load Planner

    Container dimensions follow ISO 668 standard specifications. The load optimizer uses a weight-first bin-packing heuristic. Results are illustrative — actual loading plans should be created by qualified freight planners.
